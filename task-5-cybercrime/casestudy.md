# Case Study: Phishing Attack on a College Student

This case study shows how important it is to stay cautious, verify messages, and never share sensitive information through unknown links.

Cybercrime Type: Phishing

Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers trick people into giving away sensitive information like passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source.

Taking example-
A common scenario involves a college student receiving an email that appears to be from a trusted service like Google or any bank. The message usually creates urgency—for example, claiming that the account will be suspended unless immediate action is taken.

How it happens-
First, the victim receives a convincing email or message containing a link.

Second, upon clicking the link, they are redirected to a fake website that closely resembles the original.

Third, the user enters login credentials or financial details, believing it to be genuine.

Finally, the attacker captures this information and uses it to access accounts or commit financial fraud.

Who is targeted?

College students are often targeted because they frequently use online platforms and may not always verify such messages carefully

What are the concequences?

The consequences can be serious. The student may lose money, get locked out of their accounts, or face misuse of their personal information. This can also lead to stress and long-term security risks.
